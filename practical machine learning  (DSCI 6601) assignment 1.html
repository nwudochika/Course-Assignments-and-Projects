feature_names=X.columns
model_coefficient= regressor.coef_
coefficients_dataframe=pd.DataFrame(data=model_coefficient,index=feature_names,columns=["coefficient value"])

mse = mean_squared_error(Y_test, Y_pred)

pred_diff = Y_test - Y_pred

Mean squared error (MSE) measures the amount of error in statistical models. It assesses the average squared difference between the observed and predicted values. When a model has no error, the MSE equals zero. As model error increases, its value increases. A model with less error produces more precise predictions. Only a perfect model with no error produces an MSE of zero. And that doesn’t occur in practice.

Placing emphasis on the mean square errors(MSE) on the three ratio cases, i realize that the MSE at ratio 90:10 is closer to zero compared to the others which in turn produces more precise predictions.
